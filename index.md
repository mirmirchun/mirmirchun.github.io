I am a PhD Student at the **Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST) School of AI Policy & Strategy** and serve as **Executive Director of the International Rehabilitation Forum (IRF)**, a nonprofit organization that build Physical Rehabilitation Medicine(PRM) capacity in low-resource countries.

My work bridges **AI research, social work, and systems-level capacity building** to address a critical global health equity challenge: workforce shortages and training gaps in resource-constrained health systems. 
I am especially interested in how AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), can support capacity building by expanding access to specialized knowledge, guiding contextualized learning and reflection, and helping users translate limited resources into practical decisions and locally relevant action.

## Research Focus
Many existing AI evaluations focus on model-centered performance, such as capability, accuracy, and safety violations. These measures are important, but they do not fully answer a critical question for real-world capacity-building contexts: **Do AI interactions strengthen or undermine the agency, judgment, and capacity of the people using them?**

Leveraging my background in social work, my doctoral research develops an empowerment-centered approach to AI evaluation.  

## Areas of Interest
* **AI Evaluation & Empowerment:** Developing a framework to evaluate whether LLM interactions support empowering processes, including informed choice, agency, strengths-based reasoning, resource navigation, contextual responsiveness, and sustainable capacity building.  
* **Benchmark Design for Capacity Building:** Creating benchmark tasks that reflect realistic capacity-building challenges. 
* **AI Governance and Social Justice:** Examining how AI systems may reproduce, amplify, or reduce inequities affecting underserved communities and people with disabilities. 
* **Global Health Policy:** Strengthening Physical Rehabilitation Medicine (PRM) infrastructure and specialty capacity in the low-resource settings.

### Global Leadership
Through the **IRF**, I lead systems-level strategies to strengthen rehabilitation medicine education, professional networks, and specialty training pathways in low-resource countries where rehab services and training resources are limited.

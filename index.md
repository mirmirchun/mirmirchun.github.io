I am a PhD Student at the **Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST)** School of AI Policy & Strategy. I also serve as the **Executive Director** and Board Member of the **International Rehabilitation Forum (IRF)** and as the **Primary Care Workstream Liaison** at the **World Rehabilitation Alliance (WRA)** of the WHO. My work is positioned at the intersection of **AI Alignment**, **Social Justice**, and **Global Health**. I am dedicated to ensuring that emerging AI governance frameworks are architected to protect and empower vulnerable populations, particularly within the context of disability and rehabilitation. 

### Research Interests
Leveraging my background in Social Work, my doctoral research applies **Capability Approach** to explore how AI systems within disablity and rehabilitation can be evaluated by their effectiveness in converting technological resources into substantive human functionings.

* **AI Alignment & Social Justice:** Demonstrating that data representations of human functioning must be sensitive to local clinical and geopolitical realities to ensure that AI systems reflects the lived experiences of marginalized populations.  
* **Disability & Digital Ethics:** Addressing how biased data representations and misaligned performance objectives in care-related domains risk technological displacement.
* **Global Health Policy:** Strengthening Physical Rehabilitation Medicine (PRM) infrastructure and specialty capacity in the low-resource settings.

### Global Leadership
As a **Board Member and Executive Director** of the **IRF**, I lead strategic initiatives to build Physical Rehabilitation Medicine (PRM) specialty capacity in the Global South. I believe that both technological alignment and rehabilitation services are fundamental to addressing the rising Non-Communicable Disease (NCD) burden in the Global South and the demographic challenges of an aging Global North.

*I hold a Master of Social Work (MSW) and am committed to bridging the gap between technological advancement and social equity.*
